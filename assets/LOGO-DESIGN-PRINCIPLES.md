# Kask Logo Design Principles

## Vision Statement

> *"Agents need containers, not apps."*

The Kask logo embodies this vision through a synthesis of four essential elements:

1. **The Kask (Container)** — Simple, humble rectangular vessel form
2. **Calligraphy (Art)** — Hand-drawn stroke variation, human craft
3. **The Curator's Eye (Vision)** — Observant, governing, structurally loyal
4. **Bitemporal Shadow (Perspective)** — Past and future, valid-time and transaction-time

Together: **simplicity with art, vision and sense, dynamic perspective, humble form.**

---

## Historical Lineage

The name **Kask** derives from the **gong** (觥) — a Shang Dynasty bronze ritual vessel (c. 12th–13th century BC) used for holding and pouring ritual wine. The proto-kask was a zoomorphic form combining dragon, bird, and serpent motifs into a single composite vessel.

**Modern parallel:** A kask container composes heterogeneous AI capabilities (tools, models, connectors, constraints) into a single governed protocol surface.

**Design imperative:** Honor the ancient lineage while signaling future infrastructure. The calligraphic stroke connects both — the oldest human mark-making technology meeting AI-native container infrastructure.

## The Four Elements

### 1. The Kask — Simple Container Form

> *"Like its ancestor, the modern kask is a vessel: typed, composable, and built to contain power."*

**Visual expression:**
- Rectangular amphora silhouette (box-shaped, not organic curves)
- Distinct neck and body sections
- Two side handles (functional, composable)
- Humble, infrastructure-grade geometry

**Why rectangular:**
- Signals infrastructure (containers, crates, Rust packages)
- Avoids inappropriate organic associations
- Provides flat canvas for embedded eye
- Archaeologically accurate (ancient storage vessels were often rectangular)

**Design principle:** The container must be recognizable at any scale, from favicon to billboard. Simplicity is not absence — it is precision.

---

### 2. Calligraphy — The Human Mark

> *"Simplicity with art."*

**Visual expression:**
- Varied stroke width (thick downstrokes, thin transitions)
- Pressure-sensitive line quality (simulated brush/pen)
- Organic imperfection within geometric structure
- Hand-crafted, not mechanically perfect

**Why calligraphy:**
- **Human craft** — AI infrastructure built by humans, for humans
- **Temporal mark** — Calligraphy is time made visible (stroke order, pressure, speed)
- **Restraint** — Calligraphy is disciplined art, not decoration
- **Permanence** — Ink on paper, millennia of human record-keeping

**Stroke philosophy:**
- Thick verticals (9px) = structural integrity, downstroke pressure
- Thin horizontals (5-7px) = transition, release of pressure
- Curved handles (7px) = functional grace, ability to compose
- Each stroke earns its place; nothing decorative

**Design principle:** The logo is drawn, not constructed. Every line carries the weight and release of a human hand.

---

### 3. The Curator's Eye — Vision and Sense

> *"The Curator — Kask's singular platform agent. Its loyalty to operators is structural, not configurable."*

**Visual expression:**
- Almond-shaped outline (human eye, not geometric circle)
- Upper eyelid (thick, protective)
- Lower eyelid (thin, soft)
- Iris (solid, centered)
- Pupil (dark, focused)
- Light reflection (alive, observing)
- Operator authority gap (invariant, cannot be closed)

**Why an eye:**
- **Observation** — The Curator watches all subsystems
- **Governance** — Sees violations, enforces constraints
- **Loyalty** — Eye faces outward (toward operator), not inward (toward agent goals)
- **Presence** — The platform is always observing, always available

**Eye anatomy:**
- Upper eyelid (8px) = protection, authority
- Lower eyelid (5px) = receptivity, training
- Iris (solid fill) = focused attention
- Pupil (darker) = depth, absorption
- Reflection (white dot) = life, responsiveness
- Gap in iris = operator authority invariant (structural loyalty)

**Design principle:** The eye is embedded within the vessel — the Curator is inside the platform, not external. It observes from within the container it governs.

---

### 4. Bitemporal Shadow — Dynamic Perspective

> *"Bitemporal memory — valid-time and transaction-time."*

**Visual expression:**
- Offset shadow (left, ~25px)
- Reduced opacity (15-25%)
- Same geometry as primary vessel
- Same eye, same handles, same proportions

**Why bitemporal:**
- **Valid-time** — When facts are true in the world (the shadow, the past)
- **Transaction-time** — When facts are recorded in the system (the primary, the present)
- **Dual perspective** — The platform remembers both when something happened and when it was known
- **Depth** — Flat design gains temporal dimension

**Shadow properties:**
- Offset left = past receding, memory
- Lower opacity = less present, but still visible
- Identical geometry = same truth, different time
- Never removed = bitemporality is fundamental, not optional

**Design principle:** The logo has depth without 3D effects. The shadow is not a drop shadow (decorative) — it is a temporal shadow (semantic, architectural).

## Core Design Principles

### 1. Simplicity (Recognition <400ms)

The human brain processes simple shapes in under 400 milliseconds.

**Application:**
- Single continuous vessel form
- Maximum 3 stroke weights (thick, medium, thin)
- One focal point (the eye)
- One shadow (bitemporal)

**Test:** Can you draw it from memory on a napkin in 5 seconds?

---

### 2. Memorability (Distinctive Hook)

People encounter 4,000–10,000 brand messages daily. Most vanish instantly.

**Application:**
- Calligraphic stroke variation (unusual in tech logos)
- Human eye embedded in container (unexpected)
- Bitemporal shadow (unique semantic element)
- Rectangular amphora (distinctive silhouette)

**Test:** Can someone describe it after 5-second exposure?

---

### 3. Scalability (16px to 16ft)

A logo lives at wildly different scales.

**Application:**
- Vector format (SVG)
- Minimum stroke width: 5px at base size
- Eye simplifies at small sizes (drop eyelashes, keep iris/pupil)
- Shadow drops below 64px

**Test:** Does it turn into a blob at 16×16px?

---

### 4. Versatility (All Mediums, All Contexts)

A logo must work everywhere.

**Application:**
- Light variant (black strokes on white)
- Dark variant (light strokes on dark)
- Single-color (photocopy, embroidery)
- No gradients, no effects

**Test:** Does it work when photocopied in black/white?

---

### 5. Relevance (Audience Expectations)

Logo psychology must match audience.

**Kask audience:** Rust developers, AI infra engineers, platform operators

**Application:**
- Monospace typography for wordmark
- Infrastructure geometry (rectangular, not playful)
- Technical precision (calligraphic, not sloppy)
- Cyberpunk undertone (eye + shadow = governance + telemetry)

---

### 6. Timelessness (Decades, Not Seasons)

Trends cycle rapidly. Logo must outlast design fashion.

**Application:**
- Calligraphy is ancient and permanent
- Rectangular form is archaeological and modern
- Eye is universal (not trend-driven)
- No gradients, no bevels, no effects

**Test:** Will this look dated in 10 years?

---

### 7. Balance (Visual Weight Distribution)

Composition must feel stable.

**Application:**
- Heavier at base (thick bottom strokes)
- Eye centered in lower 2/3 (visual anchor)
- Handles balance left/right
- Shadow adds weight without instability

## Design Philosophy

### Simplicity with Art

The calligraphic stroke is not decoration — it is the mark of a human hand. In a world of AI-generated perfection, the imperfect stroke signals: *humans built this*. The variation in stroke width (thick down, thin up) is the physics of writing, the rhythm of breath made visible.

### Vision and Sense

The Curator's Eye is not a camera (surveillance) — it is an eye (observation, understanding). The upper lid protects; the lower lid receives. The iris focuses; the pupil absorbs. The reflection signals life. The gap in the iris is the invariant: operator authority cannot be closed.

### Dynamic Perspective

The bitemporal shadow is not a drop shadow (depth effect) — it is a temporal shadow (architectural truth). The platform remembers when facts were true (valid-time) and when they were recorded (transaction-time). The shadow is the past; the primary is the present. Both are real.

### Humble Form

The rectangular vessel is not a monument — it is a tool. Handles signal: *I am meant to be carried, composed, used*. The geometry is infrastructure-grade (crates, containers, Rust packages). Nothing decorative. Everything functional.

## Usage Guidelines

### Clearspace

Minimum clearspace = 1× logo height on all sides.

```
    ┌─────────────┐
    │             │
    │   [LOGO]    │  ← clearspace = H
    │             │
    └─────────────┘
         H
```

### Minimum Size

| Context | Minimum Size | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Digital | 32px height | Eye must remain visible |
| Print | 10mm height | Stroke width must not collapse |
| Favicon | 16×16px | Simplified version (drop shadow, simplify eye) |
| Embroidery | 25mm width | Single-color variant |

### Do Not

- Stretch or distort proportions
- Rotate (unless designed for rotation)
- Add effects (drop shadow, bevel, glow)
- Change stroke weights
- Remove the eye
- Remove the shadow (except at small sizes)
- Place on busy backgrounds

### Always

- Use vector for print/signage
- Maintain clearspace
- Check contrast ratios (WCAG AA minimum)
- Use appropriate variant (light/dark) for background
